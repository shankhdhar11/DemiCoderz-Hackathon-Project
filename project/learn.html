üí° Food Waste Reduction Tips

1. Plan Your Meals

Create a weekly meal plan and shopping list to avoid buying unnecessary items. This helps reduce impulse purchases and ensures you use what you buy.

2. Store Food Properly

Learn the best storage methods for different foods. Proper storage can extend the life of produce, dairy, and other perishables significantly.

3. Use Your Freezer

Freeze leftovers, bread, and produce that's about to expire. Most foods can be frozen and used later, preventing waste.

4. Understand Expiry Dates

Learn the difference between "best before" and "use by" dates. Many foods are still safe to eat after the best before date.

5. Donate Surplus Food

If you have excess food that's still good, donate it to local food banks or use our donation portal to connect with NGOs.

6. Use Scraps Creatively

Vegetable peels can make stock, stale bread becomes croutons, and overripe fruits are perfect for smoothies or baking.

7. Buy Local and Seasonal

Local, seasonal produce is fresher and lasts longer. It also reduces transportation emissions and supports local farmers.

8. Portion Control

Serve smaller portions and allow people to take seconds if needed. This reduces plate waste and helps with healthy eating.
